    Letters

    There are 26 letters in English alphabet. Six of them are vowels and the rest are consonants. The vowels are: a, e, I, o, u

    Words

    Words are constructed using letters and they have to give meaning in time of speaking. Example: animal, human, cloth and cat are words

    Sentences

    Sentences are a group of words that are used to express feeling and action. We use nouns and verb to construct sentences.

    Some examples on sentences

    I go to school every day.

    I can play piano very well.

    We have to do our home work today.

    Melat like her doll very much.

    Nouns

    A noun is a kind of word that is usually the name of something such as a person, place, thing and animal.

    Nouns are classified into common and proper:

    Proper nouns
    Proper nouns (also called proper name) are specific names given to a person,thing or place.
    Common nouns
    Common nouns are nouns given to a general thing not for specific.
    Examples of proper nouns
    • Ruth
    • Chair
    • Ethiopia
    • Nile river
    Examples of common nouns
    • Girl
    • Furniture
    • Country
    • River

    Pronouns

    Pronouns are words that can replace anoun and give meaning. Pronouns can replace singular or plural nouns.

    Pronons that replace singular nouns
    • He
    • She
    • It
    • You
    Pronons that replace plural nouns
    • We
    • They

    verbs are words that describe an action.
